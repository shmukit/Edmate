Question 1Question and Options in Text Format

A scientist heats a sample of a liquid. The scientist measures the temperature of the sample and plots a graph of the temperature against time. Which statements are correct?

Between points W and X, the temperature increases and the particles move faster.

Between points X and Y, there is no change in the temperature because energy is needed to change a liquid into a gas.

Between points Y and Z, the particles move further apart.

A. 1, 2 and 3B. 1 and 2 onlyC. 1 and 3 onlyD. 2 only

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right Answer

This question requires an understanding of heating curves and the kinetic particle theory during state changes. When a substance is heated, the energy either increases the kinetic energy of particles (raising temperature) or is used to overcome intermolecular forces (changing state at a constant temperature).

Core Concept: Heating curves represent how the state and temperature of a substance change as thermal energy is added.

Analyze Step 1: Between points W and X, the graph shows a positive gradient, meaning temperature is rising. According to kinetic theory, temperature is a measure of average kinetic energy; therefore, particles move faster as they gain energy. Statement 1 is correct.

Analyze Step 2: Between points X and Y, the graph is horizontal (plateau), indicating a constant temperature despite continued heating. This occurs during a change of state. Since the starting material is a liquid, this plateau represents boiling where energy is used to break intermolecular forces to form a gas. Statement 2 is correct.

Analyze Step 3: Between points Y and Z, the substance is now a gas being heated further. Gases expand to fill containers, and as the temperature of a gas increases, the particles move with even greater speed and move further apart from one another. Statement 3 is correct.

Final Correct Answer: Since statements 1, 2, and 3 are all correct, the answer is A.

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)

Option A is correct because it acknowledges that all three provided statements accurately describe the physical processes occurring at different stages of the heating curve. Between W and X, the liquid’s temperature rises as kinetic energy increases. Between X and Y, the substance undergoes a phase change (boiling) where potential energy increases but temperature remains constant. Between Y and Z, the resulting gas particles move further apart.

Option B is incorrect because it excludes statement 3. While statements 1 and 2 are correct, statement 3 is also a scientifically accurate description of the behavior of gas particles being heated beyond their boiling point.

Option C is incorrect because it excludes statement 2. Statement 2 correctly identifies that the plateau in a heating curve for a liquid represents the boiling point where energy is consumed for the phase transition rather than temperature increase.

Option D is incorrect because it suggests only statement 2 is correct, ignoring the valid kinetic descriptions provided in statements 1 and 3 regarding particle speed and spacing.

C.Statement 1: X is a mixture with the formula FeS.Statement 2: X is a compound with the formula FeS.

D.Statement 1: X is a mixture with the formula FeSO₄.Statement 2: X is a compound with the formula FeSO₄.

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right Answer

The question tests the distinction between mixtures and compounds. Mixtures can contain elements in any proportion, whereas compounds have a fixed ratio of elements by mass, determined by their chemical formula and relative atomic masses.

Core Concept:A compound has a fixed composition by mass, while a mixture can have any composition.

Analyze Step 1:Calculate the mass ratio of a common iron–sulfur compound, FeS.The mass of Fe is 56 and the mass of S is 32.The ratio Fe : S is 56 : 32.

Analyze Step 2:Simplify the ratio 56 : 32.Both numbers are divisible by 8.56 ÷ 8 = 732 ÷ 8 = 4The fixed mass ratio for the compound FeS is therefore 7 : 4.

Analyze Step 3:Evaluate the options.A mixture can exist in a 7 : 4 ratio because mixtures can have any ratio.A compound of iron and sulfur (FeS) must exist in the fixed ratio 7 : 4.Therefore, both statements in row B can be correct.

Final Correct Answer:The answer is B.

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)

Option A is incorrect.While X can be a mixture with a 4 : 7 ratio, a compound of iron and sulfur has a fixed ratio of 7 : 4. A 4 : 7 ratio does not correspond to the stoichiometric composition of FeS.

Option B is correct.A mixture can be formed by mixing 7 g of iron with 4 g of sulfur, as mixtures can exist in any ratio. At the same time, the compound iron(II) sulfide (FeS) has a fixed mass ratio of 7 : 4 based on the relative atomic masses 56 : 32. Thus, both statements are possible.

Option C is incorrect.The term “formula” (such as FeS) applies only to compounds, where elements are chemically bonded in a fixed ratio. A mixture does not have a chemical formula.

Option D is incorrect.The question states that X contains iron and sulfur only. The formula FeSO₄ contains oxygen, which contradicts the information given. In addition, a mixture cannot be defined by a single chemical formula.

Particle | Electrons | Neutrons | ProtonsW | 28 | 22 | 25X | 28 | 23 | 25Y | 30 | 26 | 26Z | 28 | 26 | 26

A. W and XB. W and ZC. X and ZD. Y and Z

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right Answer

This question tests the definition of isotopes. Isotopes are atoms of the same element that have the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons.

Core Concept:The identity of an element is determined solely by its proton number. Isotopes must have identical proton numbers but different neutron numbers.

Analyze Step 1:Examine the proton numbers in the table.W and X both have 25 protons.Y and Z both have 26 protons.

Analyze Step 2:Check for differences in neutrons between particles with the same number of protons.For W and X:W has 22 neutrons, X has 23 neutrons.They have the same proton number but different neutron numbers, so they are isotopes.

Analyze Step 3:Check the pair Y and Z.Y has 26 neutrons and Z also has 26 neutrons.Since both the proton and neutron numbers are the same, these are not different isotopes.

Analyze Step 4:Re-evaluate the requirement for isotopes.Isotopes must differ in neutron number. Only W and X satisfy this condition.

Final Correct Answer:The answer is A.

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)

Option A is correct.W and X both have 25 protons, identifying them as the same element. They differ in neutron number (22 and 23), which is the defining feature of isotopes.

Option B is incorrect.W has 25 protons while Z has 26 protons. Different proton numbers mean different elements, so they cannot be isotopes.

Option C is incorrect.X has 25 protons and Z has 26 protons. Since the proton numbers are different, they are different elements.

Option D is incorrect.Y and Z both have 26 protons and 26 neutrons. Their nuclei are identical, so they are the same isotope. The different numbers of electrons mean they are different ions, not isotopes.

Option A is incorrect because the empirical and molecular formulae of a compound can be identical.This occurs when the actual number of atoms in a molecule is already in its simplest whole-number ratio, such as in water (H₂O) or methane (CH₄).

Option B is correct because both ethyne (C₂H₂) and benzene (C₆H₆) have a 1 : 1 ratio of carbon to hydrogen atoms.When simplified to the lowest whole-number ratio, both yield the empirical formula CH.

Option C is incorrect because it describes the definition of an empirical formula, not a molecular formula.The molecular formula shows the actual number of each type of atom present in a molecule, which is often a multiple of the empirical formula.
