Question and Options in Text Format
In which changes do the particles move further apart? 

1 A gas is heated from 0°C to 25°C. 
2 Pressure is applied to a gas at a constant temperature. 
3 Steam condenses to form water. 
4 Water evaporates at room temperature. 

🅰️ 1 and 2 
🅱️ 1 and 4 
C 2 and 3 
D 3 and 4 

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right Answer
The question asks to identify physical changes where the distance between particles increases. This occurs when thermal energy increases particle movement (expansion) or when a substance changes to a less dense state of matter (liquid to gas). 

Core Concept: The kinetic particle theory states that the distance between particles increases during expansion (heating) and phase changes from liquid to gas (evaporation/boiling), while it decreases during compression or cooling/condensation. 

Analyze Step 1: When a gas is heated from 0°C to 25°C, the particles gain kinetic energy and move faster, leading to thermal expansion where particles move further apart. 
Intermediate result: Change 1 fits the criteria. 

Analyze Step 2: Applying pressure to a gas forces the particles closer together into a smaller volume. 
Intermediate result: Change 2 does not fit the criteria. 

Analyze Step 3: Condensation is the transition from a gas to a liquid. Particles in a gas are far apart, whereas in a liquid, they are close together. Therefore, particles move closer. 
Intermediate result: Change 3 does not fit the criteria. 

Analyze Step 4: Evaporation is the transition from a liquid to a gas. Particles at the surface of the liquid gain enough energy to escape into the gaseous state, where they are significantly further apart. 
Result: Change 4 fits the criteria. 

Final Correct Answer: 🅱️

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)
Option 🅰️ is incorrect because while heating a gas (1) increases particle distance, applying pressure (2) decreases it by forcing particles into a smaller volume. 
Option 🅱️ is correct because both heating a gas (1) and evaporation (4) result in particles moving further apart due to increased kinetic energy and phase transition to a less dense state, respectively. 
Option C is incorrect because applying pressure (2) and condensation (3) both result in particles moving closer together. 
Option D is incorrect because condensation (3) decreases the distance between particles, even though evaporation (4) increases it. 

🧠 Concept Gap Analysis and Flashcards
Option 🅰️ Gap: Misunderstanding the effect of pressure on gas volume and particle proximity. 
Flashcard 1: How does increasing pressure affect the distance between gas particles? Back: It decreases the distance as particles are forced closer together. 
Flashcard 2: What happens to the volume of a gas when it is compressed? Back: The volume decreases because the space between particles is reduced. 

Option 🅱️ Gap: None (Correct selection). 
Flashcard 1: Define evaporation in terms of particle distance. Back: Evaporation is the process where liquid particles move much further apart to become a gas. 
Flashcard 2: Why do gas particles move further apart when heated? Back: They gain kinetic energy and move more vigorously, causing the gas to expand. 

Option C Gap: Confusion between condensation and evaporation; misunderstanding that pressure reduces volume. 
Flashcard 1: Does condensation increase or decrease particle distance? Back: Decrease; particles move from far apart (gas) to close together (liquid). 
Flashcard 2: Why is pressure used to liquefy gases? Back: Because pressure forces particles closer together until attractive forces take over. 

Option D Gap: Incorrectly identifying condensation as a process where particles spread out. 
Flashcard 1: Compare particle spacing in steam vs. liquid water. Back: Particles in steam are far apart and move randomly; in water, they are close together and touching. 
Flashcard 2: Is energy absorbed or released during condensation? Back: Energy is released as particles move closer together and form bonds/attractive forces.
