1Question and Options in Text Format

Which physical changes are both exothermic?

1 A condensation and evaporation2 B evaporation and melting3 C freezing and condensation4 D melting and freezing

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right Answer

To identify exothermic physical changes, one must determine which processes involve the release of thermal energy to the surroundings as particles move closer together and form stronger attractive forces.

Core Concept:Exothermic changes involve the release of heat energy (–ΔH) when a substance changes state, typically occurring when moving from a more disordered state (gas or liquid) to a more ordered state (liquid or solid).

Analyze Step 1: Identify the energy profile of changes of state.Processes like melting, evaporation, and sublimation require energy input to overcome intermolecular forces and are therefore endothermic.

Analyze Step 2: Identify processes where energy is released.When a gas turns into a liquid (condensation) or a liquid turns into a solid (freezing), particles lose kinetic energy and bond/attractive forces are established, releasing energy to the surroundings.

Analyze Step 3: Evaluate the options based on these definitions.Option C includes both freezing and condensation, both of which are processes that release heat.

Final Correct Answer:The correct answer is C.

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)

Option A is incorrect because while condensation is exothermic (heat released), evaporation is an endothermic process that requires the absorption of energy from the surroundings to overcome intermolecular forces.

Option B is incorrect because both evaporation and melting are endothermic processes; they require an intake of energy to increase the distance between particles and overcome attractive forces.

Option C is correct because both freezing and condensation are exothermic; as particles lose kinetic energy and move closer together to form a liquid or solid, energy is released into the environment.

Option D is incorrect because melting is an endothermic process, even though freezing is exothermic, making the pair inconsistent with the requirement of the question.

🧠 Concept Gap Analysis and Flashcards

Option A Gap:Confusing the cooling effect felt during evaporation (surroundings lose heat) with the energy change of the substance itself (substance gains heat).

Flashcard 1:Is evaporation an endothermic or exothermic process?Back: It is endothermic because the substance absorbs energy to overcome intermolecular forces.

Flashcard 2:Why does condensation release energy?Back: Because particles move from a high-energy gaseous state to a lower-energy liquid state, releasing the difference as heat.

Flashcard 1:Does melting require or release energy?Back: It requires energy (endothermic) to break the regular lattice structure of a solid.

Flashcard 2:What is the sign of the enthalpy change (ΔH) for an endothermic process?Back: The sign is positive (+ΔH).

Option C Gap:No gap; correctly identifies that moving toward a more condensed state (solid or liquid) releases energy.

Flashcard 1:Define an exothermic physical change.Back: A change of state where heat is released to the surroundings, such as freezing or condensing.

Flashcard 2:List two examples of exothermic state changes.Back: Freezing and condensation.

Option D Gap:Failing to distinguish between processes that require heat (melting) and those that release heat (freezing).

Flashcard 1:Why is melting considered endothermic?Back: Energy must be absorbed from the surroundings to provide particles with enough kinetic energy to break out of fixed positions.

Flashcard 2:What happens to the temperature of the surroundings during an exothermic reaction?Back: The temperature of the surroundings increases as heat is released.
