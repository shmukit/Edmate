1Question and Options in Text FormatIn which changes do the particles move further apart?

1 A gas is heated from 0°C to 25°C.2 Pressure is applied to a gas at a constant temperature.3 Steam condenses to form water.4 Water evaporates at room temperature.

A. 1 and 2B. 1 and 4C. 2 and 3D. 3 and 4

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right AnswerThe question asks to identify physical changes where the distance between particles increases. This occurs when thermal energy increases particle movement (expansion) or when a substance changes to a less dense state of matter (liquid to gas).

Core Concept: The kinetic particle theory states that the distance between particles increases during expansion (heating) and phase changes from liquid to gas (evaporation/boiling), while it decreases during compression or cooling/condensation.

Analyze Step 1: When a gas is heated from 0°C to 25°C, the particles gain kinetic energy and move faster, leading to thermal expansion where particles move further apart.Intermediate result: Change 1 fits the criteria.

Analyze Step 2: Applying pressure to a gas forces the particles closer together into a smaller volume.Intermediate result: Change 2 does not fit the criteria.

Analyze Step 3: Condensation is the transition from a gas to a liquid. Particles in a gas are far apart, whereas in a liquid, they are close together. Therefore, particles move closer.Intermediate result: Change 3 does not fit the criteria.

Analyze Step 4: Evaporation is the transition from a liquid to a gas. Particles at the surface of the liquid gain enough energy to escape into the gaseous state, where they are significantly further apart.Result: Change 4 fits the criteria.

Final Correct Answer: B

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)Option A is incorrect because while heating a gas (1) increases particle distance, applying pressure (2) decreases it by forcing particles into a smaller volume.Option B is correct because both heating a gas (1) and evaporation (4) result in particles moving further apart due to increased kinetic energy and phase transition to a less dense state, respectively.Option C is incorrect because applying pressure (2) and condensation (3) both result in particles moving closer together.Option D is incorrect because condensation (3) decreases the distance between particles, even though evaporation (4) increases it.

Analyze Step 4: Conclude that A is the only correct statement.

Final Correct Answer: A

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right AnswerThe question asks for the specific number of ions in a given mass of an ionic compound.The method involves calculating the moles of the compound, determining the number of ions per formula unit, and multiplying by Avogadro's constant.

Analyze Step 1: Calculate the relative formula mass (Mᵣ) of sodium sulfate, Na₂SO₄. Using the periodic table: Na = 23.0, S = 32.1, O = 16.0.Mᵣ = (2 × 23.0) + 32.1 + (4 × 16.0) = 46.0 + 32.1 + 64.0 = 142.1.Intermediate result: Mᵣ = 142.1.

Analyze Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of Na₂SO₄ in 30 g.Moles = Mass / Mᵣ = 30 / 142.1 ≈ 0.2111 mol.Intermediate result: Moles ≈ 0.2111.

Analyze Step 4: Calculate the actual number of ions.Number = Moles × 6.02 × 10²³ = 0.4222 × 6.02 × 10²³ ≈ 2.54 × 10²³.Result: Option B is correct.
