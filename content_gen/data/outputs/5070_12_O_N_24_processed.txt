Question 1Question and Options in Text Format

Helium gas and argon gas are mixed in a closed container at room temperature and pressure (r.t.p.). What happens when the two gases are in the container?

A Argon and helium atoms become evenly mixed throughout the container even though they have different masses.

B Argon and helium atoms both move towards the bottom of the container. The argon atoms settle more quickly because they are larger and heavier.

C Argon and helium atoms both move towards the bottom of the container. The helium atoms settle more quickly because they are smaller and lighter.

D Argon atoms move to the bottom of the container because they are heavier. Helium atoms move to the top of the container because they are lighter.

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right Answer

The movement and mixing of gases are governed by the kinetic particle theory, which states that gas particles are in constant, random motion. This motion leads to diffusion, ensuring that gases will eventually form a uniform mixture regardless of their relative atomic masses.

Core Concept: The Kinetic Particle Theory and Diffusion in gases.

Analyze Step 1: Identify the state of matter and the nature of the particles. Helium and argon are noble gases existing as individual atoms in constant, random motion.

Analyze Step 2: Consider the effect of gravity versus kinetic energy. At room temperature and pressure, the kinetic energy of gas particles is sufficient to overcome the pull of gravity, preventing them from “settling” at the bottom.

Analyze Step 3: Determine the result of random motion. Because particles move randomly in all directions, they spread out from areas of high concentration to low concentration. This process, diffusion, continues until the particles are evenly distributed.

Final Correct Answer: The correct answer is A.

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)

Option A is correct because the kinetic particle theory dictates that gas atoms are in continuous random motion, leading to diffusion. This process ensures that helium and argon atoms will eventually be distributed uniformly throughout the available volume of the container, regardless of the fact that argon atoms are significantly heavier than helium atoms.

Option B is incorrect because it suggests that gas particles settle due to gravity. In reality, the thermal energy at room temperature provides enough kinetic energy for the atoms to move rapidly and randomly, which overrides the effect of gravity that would otherwise cause “settling.”

Option C is incorrect for the same reason as Option B. While it correctly identifies helium as being lighter, it wrongly assumes that gas particles in a closed container at r.t.p. will settle at the bottom like solids in a liquid.

Option D is incorrect because it describes a separation based on density (like oil and water). While argon is denser than helium, the constant random motion of gas atoms prevents them from forming distinct layers in a small container; they mix thoroughly instead.

The relative atomic mass (Aᵣ) is the weighted average of the masses of the isotopes based on their relative abundance.

Core Concept: Calculation of Relative Atomic Mass (Aᵣ).

Analyze Step 1: Identify the data from the graph.Isotope A: Mass = 20, Abundance = 1.Isotope B: Mass = 22, Abundance = 5.

Analyze Step 2: Determine the total abundance. Total = 1 + 5 = 6.

Analyze Step 3: Apply the weighted average formula:Aᵣ = ((mass₁ × abundance₁) + (mass₂ × abundance₂)) / total abundanceAᵣ = ((20 × 1) + (22 × 5)) / 6

Analyze Step 4: Complete the calculation:Aᵣ = (20 + 110) / 6 = 130 / 6 ≈ 21.666…Rounding to one decimal place gives 21.7.

Final Correct Answer: The correct answer is D.

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)

Option A is incorrect because it is the simple arithmetic mean (20 + 22) / 2 = 21. This fails to account for the fact that there are five times as many atoms of mass 22 as there are of mass 20.

Option B is incorrect and might result from a calculation error or misreading the abundance of the mass 22 isotope as lower than it actually is.

Option C is incorrect. A value of 21.5 would imply a 1:3 ratio of abundances, but the graph clearly shows a 1:5 ratio.

Option D is correct. By using the weighted average formula with the ratio of 1 (for mass 20) to 5 (for mass 22), the calculated value is approximately 21.7, reflecting that the average mass is pulled much closer to 22 because that isotope is more abundant.

Which statement about electrical conductivity is correct?

A Covalent compounds, such as glucose, conduct when molten or dissolved in water.

B Dilute acids, such as sulfuric acid, conduct because all the ions are free to move.

C Ionic compounds, such as sodium chloride, conduct due to movement of electrons.

D Metals, such as copper, conduct due to movement of positive ions.

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right Answer

Electrical conductivity requires the presence of mobile charge carriers, which can be either delocalised electrons or free-moving ions.

Core Concept: Requirements for electrical conductivity in different types of substances.

Analyze Step 1: Evaluate covalent compounds. Glucose consists of neutral molecules. Even when dissolved or molten, there are no ions or delocalised electrons to carry a charge.

Analyze Step 2: Evaluate ionic compounds. In solid form, ions are fixed. When molten or aqueous, the lattice breaks and ions are free to move. However, they conduct via ions, not electrons.

Analyze Step 3: Evaluate metals. Metals conduct in solid and liquid states because they have a “sea” of delocalised electrons that can move, not positive ions (which are fixed in a lattice).

Analyze Step 4: Evaluate dilute acids. Acids like sulfuric acid ionise in water to produce H⁺ and SO₄²⁻ ions. Because these ions are free to move in the solution, they conduct electricity.

Final Correct Answer: The correct answer is B.

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)

Option A is incorrect because glucose is a simple molecular covalent compound. It does not form ions in water or when molten, and it has no delocalised electrons, making it a non-conductor.

Option B is correct. Sulfuric acid is an electrolyte. In aqueous solution, it dissociates into ions (H⁺ and SO₄²⁻). These mobile ions act as charge carriers, allowing the solution to conduct electricity.

Option C is incorrect because it misidentifies the charge carrier. Ionic compounds conduct electricity via the movement of ions (cations and anions) toward electrodes, not via electrons.

Option D is incorrect because it also misidentifies the charge carrier. In metals, the positive ions (cations) are held in fixed positions within the giant metallic lattice; it is the delocalised electrons that move and conduct electricity.

Which substance is not malleable and conducts electricity by the movement of electrons through a lattice of atoms?

Detailed Explanation of the Question and Right Answer

The question requires identifying a substance based on its mechanical property (lack of malleability) and its mechanism of electrical conductivity (electron flow through a lattice).

This involves differentiating between metallic, ionic, and giant covalent structures.

Core Concept: Relationship between structure, bonding, and physical properties in giant structures.

Analyze Step 1: Identify the conductivity mechanism. The movement of electrons through a lattice of atoms is characteristic of metals and certain giant covalent structures like graphite.

This eliminates aqueous sodium chloride, which conducts via ions.

Analyze Step 2: Examine the property of malleability. Metals are malleable because their layers of positive ions can slide over each other without breaking the metallic bond.

Graphite, however, is brittle (not malleable) because although it has delocalised electrons between layers, the layers are held by weak forces and the covalent bonds within layers are rigid.

Analyze Step 3: Match both criteria. Gold is a metal, so it is malleable and conducts via electrons.

Graphite is a giant covalent structure that is not malleable but does conduct electricity via delocalised electrons.

Final Correct Answer: The correct answer is C.

Option Wise Explanation (Detailed)

Option A is incorrect because aqueous sodium chloride is an electrolyte.

While it conducts electricity, the charge is carried by the movement of mobile sodium and chloride ions, not by electrons moving through a lattice of atoms.

Furthermore, “malleability” is a term used for solids, and an aqueous solution is a liquid.

Option B is incorrect because gold is a transition metal.

Metals are characterised by being highly malleable and ductile because the non-directional metallic bonds allow layers of atoms to slide.

While it does conduct via electrons, it fails the “not malleable” criterion.

Option C is correct because graphite has a giant covalent structure consisting of hexagonal layers of carbon atoms.

Each carbon atom is bonded to three others, leaving one delocalised electron per atom to move freely between the layers, allowing for conductivity.

Because it is a giant covalent structure, it is brittle and not malleable.

Option D is incorrect because solid sodium chloride does not conduct electricity at all.

In the solid state, the ions are fixed in a giant ionic lattice and are not free to move.

Additionally, ionic solids are brittle, not malleable, but they fail the conductivity requirement.

To find the greatest mass of oxygen, we must calculate the total moles of oxygen atoms in each sample.

Multiplying 0.2 by 9 gives 1.8 moles of oxygen atoms, which is the highest value among the choices.
