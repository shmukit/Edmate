# 🧠 Edmate Pedagogy & Learning Science Framework

> **Transparency Report**: This document outlines the pedagogical theories, learning science principles, and academic research that govern how Edmate generates content.

Edmate is not just an AI wrapper; it is a **Content Infrastructure Factory** designed to encode proven learning science into structured data. We follow a "Default ON" philosophy: every question generated by Edmate is infused with pedagogy so that teachers and platforms don't have to be learning scientists to produce world-class assessments.

---

## 🏛️ The 7 Pillars of Edmate Pedagogy

### 1. Retrieval Practice (Practice Testing)
**The Principle**: Actively recalling information from memory strengthens neural pathways and makes knowledge more durable than passive re-reading.
- **How Edmate implements it**: By generating multiple low-stakes question variants (MCQs, T/F, Cloze) from the same concept.
- **Academic Source**: [Dunlosky et al. (2013)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/1529100612453266) - *Improving Students' Learning With Effective Learning Techniques.*

### 2. Spaced Repetition (Distributed Practice)
**The Principle**: Information is better retained when review sessions are spaced out over time.
- **How Edmate implements it**: Every flashcard generated includes SM-2 algorithm metadata (`ease_factor`, `interval`) to enable platforms to schedule reviews perfectly.
- **Academic Source**: [Cepeda et al. (2006)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16784440/) - *Distributed practice in verbal recall tasks: A review and quantitative synthesis.*

### 3. Interleaving
**The Principle**: Mixing different topics or problem types within a single session improves the ability to discriminate between concepts.
- **How Edmate implements it**: Every item is tagged with `concept_links[]` and `interleaving_tags[]`, allowing platforms to programmatically compose "mixed-topic" quiz sets.
- **Academic Source**: [Rohrer & Taylor (2007)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/BF03193426) - *The effects of overlearning and distributed practice on the retention of mathematics knowledge.*

### 4. Bloom's Taxonomy
**The Principle**: Educational objectives vary in cognitive complexity, from basic recall to complex evaluation.
- **How Edmate implements it**: Every question is tagged with a Bloom's level (`Remember` → `Create`). The engine targets a balanced distribution of levels across a curriculum.
- **Academic Source**: [Bloom et al. (1956)](https://www.google.com/search?q=Bloom+Taxonomy+1956+original+text) - *Taxonomy of Educational Objectives.*

### 5. Elaborative Interrogation
**The Principle**: Asking "why" an answer is correct (and why distractors are wrong) deepens understanding.
- **How Edmate implements it**: Option-level granularity. Edmate provides an explanation for **every** distractor, identifying the specific "Concept Gap" or misconception.
- **Academic Source**: [Pressley et al. (1987)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/acp.2350010104) - *Elaborative interrogation: Facilitating the recall of factual information.*

### 6. Cognitive Load Theory
**The Principle**: Working memory is limited; instruction should be structured to manage "extraneous" load.
- **How Edmate implements it**: Layered Explanations. Content is provided in 3 layers: `core_concept` (low load), `detailed_logic` (medium load), and `scaffolded_steps`.
- **Academic Source**: [Sweller (1988)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1207/s15516709cog1202_4) - *Cognitive load during problem solving: Effects on learning.*

### 7. Formative vs. Summative Design
**The Principle**: Assessments serve different roles—feedback for learning (formative) or evaluation of learning (summative).
- **How Edmate implements it**: The `assessment_role` metadata tag directs platforms to surface content in the right context (e.g., hiding hints in summative mode).
- **Academic Source**: [Black & Wiliam (1998)](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/0969594980050103) - *Assessment and Classroom Learning.*

## 🛡️ High-Integrity Assessments (HIA): AI Resilience

In the era of Generative AI, Edmate introduces the **HIA Framework** to ensure assessment integrity. These are "Authentic Assessments" designed so that students must demonstrate genuine understanding rather than just copy-pasting from an AI.

### HIA Strategies
*   **Critical Evaluation (AI Critique)**: The student must identify errors in a deliberately flawed AI answer.
*   **Parametric Randomization (Isomorphic Variants)**: Every student receives a unique numerical/contextual version of the same concept.
*   **Process-over-Product (Scaffolding)**: Breaking a task into mandatory step-by-step intellectual milestones.

**Research Basis**: [Lodge et al. (2023)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41539-023-00162-w) - *Assessment in the age of generative artificial intelligence.*
